Learning Jazz Guitar, Quietly

A small, anonymous page with a simple path: a few chords, a few shapes, a few minutes a day.

1. Keep it ridiculously small

Instead of trying to “learn jazz”, aim for something much smaller: one tune, a few chords, and a way to play simple melodies over them.

  • Pick one song (e.g. a simple standard you like).
  • Learn the basic chords in the easiest positions.
  • Learn the melody on the top 2–3 strings.
One tune Slow practice Short sessions

2. A tiny daily routine

10–15 minutes is enough:

  • 2 min – tune, relax shoulders, breathe.
  • 5 min – play the chords slowly, in time.
  • 5–8 min – play the melody, then alternate chord–melody.

If you feel stuck or tired, stop. Small, repeatable practice beats heroic effort.

What to focus on first

Ignore theory at the beginning if it overwhelms you.

  • Sound: a warm, relaxed tone.
  • Time: slow and steady, with a soft metronome or backing track.
  • Taste: leave space, don’t fill every second with notes.

Simple “next steps” list

  • Learn 3 jazz chords you can switch between cleanly.
  • Learn 1 simple standard you like the sound of.
  • Record yourself once a week, just for you.

No pressure, no audience, no rush.

This page is anonymous on purpose. You don’t have to be impressive to make music. You just have to come back to the guitar tomorrow.
